
# INDIAN TENANT RIGHTS
Under Indian law, tenants have several basic rights including:

1. Right to peaceful possession: Once a property is rented, the tenant has the right to peaceful possession without interference from the landlord.

2. Right to essential services: Landlords cannot cut off essential services like electricity, water supply, etc. to force a tenant to vacate.

3. Right against eviction: A tenant cannot be evicted without following proper legal procedure and notice periods as specified in the Rent Control Acts of various states.

4. Right to receipt: Tenants have the right to demand and receive rent receipts for all payments made.

5. Right to repairs: Tenants can request necessary repairs for the property's structural elements, though specific maintenance responsibilities vary by state laws and rental agreements.

6. Right against arbitrary rent increase: Most states have regulations against arbitrary rent increases without proper notice.

7. Right to privacy: Landlords must provide notice before visiting the property, though specific requirements vary by state.

8. Right to return of security deposit: Upon vacating the premises in good condition, tenants have the right to receive their security deposit back.

# INDIAN CONSTITUTIONAL RIGHTS
The Constitution of India guarantees several fundamental rights to all citizens:

1. Article 14: Right to Equality - Equal protection of laws to all persons.

2. Article 15: Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.

3. Article 19: Protection of certain rights regarding freedom of speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, and profession.

4. Article 21: Protection of life and personal liberty - No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law.

5. Article 21A: Right to Education - Free and compulsory education for all children between the ages of 6 and 14.

6. Article 22: Protection against arrest and detention in certain cases.

7. Articles 23 & 24: Right against exploitation, prohibiting human trafficking, forced labor, and child labor.

8. Articles 25-28: Freedom of religion and right to freely profess, practice and propagate any religion.

9. Articles 29-30: Cultural and educational rights of minorities.

10. Article 32: Right to Constitutional Remedies - Right to move the Supreme Court for enforcement of Fundamental Rights.
